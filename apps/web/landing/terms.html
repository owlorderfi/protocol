What OwlOrderFi is

A set of open-source smart contracts on EVM-compatible chains (currently Base mainnet and Polygon PoS) that allow you to place limit, DCA, TWAP and ladder orders against Uniswap V3 pools. A keeper bot watches the chain and executes your signed orders when their conditions are met.

Technology interface, not a financial service

The OwlOrderFi maintainer (currently an individual; in the future a registered legal entity) operates the frontend at owlorderfi.com as a technological interface. The maintainer does NOT provide investment services, brokerage, portfolio management, asset custody, fiat on/off ramp, or any other regulated financial service. The maintainer's service relationship with any user is limited to that user's interaction with owlorderfi.com itself; orders execute based on each user's own wallet signature, and the maintainer cannot intervene in or reverse a signed order once placed.

Services not offered in EU, UK, US, or sanctioned jurisdictions

The OwlOrderFi frontend is not directed at, and does not offer services to, residents of: the member states of the European Union, the United Kingdom, the United States of America, or jurisdictions subject to international sanctions (currently the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, Iran, Cuba, Syria, Belarus, the Russian Federation, and Crimea). Visitors connecting from these jurisdictions receive an HTTP 451 response indicating the frontend is unavailable to them. No marketing, on-boarding, support, or other commercial activity is directed at users in these jurisdictions.

The basis for each restriction, in brief:

  • EU member states — under MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation, in force June 2024; transitional period ends 1 July 2026), a non-custodial frontend that collects a protocol fee can be classified as a Crypto-Asset Service Provider (CASP) requiring formal authorization, which is not economically feasible for a solo operator at this stage.
  • United Kingdom — the FCA financial promotion regime (in force October 2023) restricts how crypto services can be marketed to UK consumers; a parallel UK CASP framework is converging on MiCA-style authorization requirements.
  • United States — SEC enforcement against DEX frontend operators (Uniswap Wells Notice; cases involving Coinbase, Kraken, Binance) plus state-level money transmitter regimes (notably NY BitLicense and California's DFPI framework).
  • Sanctioned jurisdictions — combined EU, US, and UK sanctions regimes apply to the operator regardless of where the operating entity is domiciled.

The service that the OwlOrderFi maintainer operates — including the frontend at owlorderfi.com, the keeper bot that executes signed orders, and any user support, marketing, or other interaction with users — is not offered to residents of the jurisdictions listed above.

This stance may change as the regulatory environment evolves.

Non-custodial — we never hold your funds

Your tokens stay in your wallet until the moment of execution. You sign each order with your own private key. The contracts pull tokens via standard ERC-20 allowances; you can revoke allowances at any time directly with your wallet, independent of this site.

No financial advice

Nothing here is investment, tax, or legal advice. Triggers, quotes, distance-to-trigger, suggested trigger values, fee tier recommendations — all of it is informational. You alone decide what to sign. Crypto markets are volatile; orders may fill at unfavourable prices or fail entirely.

Risks you accept by using this site

  • Smart-contract bugs.
  • Front-running, slippage, MEV — partially mitigated by the signed minimum output and private-mempool routing where available.
  • Keeper unavailability — orders may not execute promptly during outages.
  • Network congestion — gas spikes may cause break-even skips.
  • Stablecoin de-pegs.
  • As with any DeFi contract, the worst-case loss is bounded by the ERC-20 allowance you've granted — typically the size of your open orders. Granting an unlimited allowance raises that ceiling; you can revoke or adjust the allowance directly from your wallet at any time.

Source & transparency

The protocol code (frontend, API, keeper, and smart contracts) is open source under BSL 1.1. Security artifacts and history live in the repository. Each order's on-chain receipt is surfaced in the app once the order executes.

Browser storage & third parties

We do not run analytics, advertising trackers, or fingerprinting scripts. We do not set HTTP cookies of our own. The app relies on your browser's localStorage to remember a small set of essential preferences (sign-in session, active tab, sort order, chain filter, legal acknowledgement). Connecting a wallet activates wallet-connection libraries (RainbowKit, WalletConnect/Reown) which may load their own resources from their infrastructure — see their privacy policies for details. Under ePrivacy Directive Art. 5(3), these local-storage items qualify as strictly necessary for the service you requested, so no separate consent is required.
