Privacy Policy

Effective date: 2026-05-25 · Last reviewed: 2026-05-29

1. Who we are (Data Controller)

OwlOrderFi is operated by an individual developer based in Romania (EU). For privacy-related inquiries, including requests to exercise your rights below, contact: privacy@owlorderfi.com. There is no Data Protection Officer requirement — we do not process special-category data and do not perform large-scale systematic monitoring (GDPR Art. 37).

2. What we collect

  • Wallet address you connect with. Treated as personal data under GDPR (pseudonymous identifier). Used to identify your account, retrieve your orders, and verify ownership of orders you sign.
  • Order metadata you submit — token pair, amounts, trigger price, schedule (for DCA/TWAP), slippage tolerance, your signature, the deadline. Stored so the keeper can execute when conditions are met.
  • Execution records — slice fills, failure reasons, transaction hashes. Generated by the keeper, stored alongside your order.
  • Authentication session — a hash of the JWT issued after you sign a sign-in message, plus the session expiry. No IP address or User-Agent is stored with the session. The JWT itself lives in your browser's localStorage; cleared on sign-out.
  • Reverse-proxy access logs — standard HTTP request lines logged at the edge (IP address, timestamp, path, response status, User-Agent). Rotated automatically (oldest discarded after ~5 log files / 250 MB). Used only for abuse mitigation and rate limiting.

We do not collect: real name, email (unless you initiate contact with us), phone number, government IDs, date of birth, precise geolocation, behavioural analytics, or advertising identifiers.

3. Legal basis (GDPR Art. 6)

  • Contract performance (Art. 6(1)(b)): processing your wallet address, orders, signatures, and executions is necessary to provide the service you requested.
  • Legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f)): server logs for abuse prevention and rate limiting. Retention is short.

4. Retention

  • Orders + executions: retained indefinitely for audit and historical accuracy. You can request deletion at any time (see rights below).
  • Authentication sessions: expire after a fixed window (typically 30 days). Expired session rows are cleaned from the database periodically.
  • Reverse-proxy access logs: rotated by file size (5 files × 50 MB), oldest discarded automatically. Effective retention depends on traffic volume — typically days to weeks.
  • On-chain data: anything we submit to a public blockchain (execution transactions) is permanent and outside our control. The wallet address is publicly visible on the chain regardless of our retention.

5. Recipients & sub-processors

  • Hosting: self-hosted infrastructure (server in EU). No third-party cloud platform processes your data.
  • RPC providers: when you submit an order, transaction data is sent to Ethereum-compatible RPC endpoints (Alchemy, public RPCs). They see the transaction payload but not your sign-in session.
  • Wallet libraries: connecting your wallet loads RainbowKit / WalletConnect (Reown) resources from their infrastructure. See their own privacy policies.
  • Block explorers: when we render a "tx ↗" link, clicking it opens an explorer page (Etherscan, Basescan, Arbiscan) which is operated by a third party and subject to their privacy policy.

6. International transfers

All our infrastructure is hosted in the EU. Third-party services listed above (Alchemy, WalletConnect, block explorers) are operated globally and may process data in non-EU countries; data sent to them is the transaction payload only, which is by design public on the blockchain.

7. Your rights (GDPR Chapter III)

You have the right to:

  • Access (Art. 15) — request a copy of the data we hold about you.
  • Rectification (Art. 16) — correct inaccurate data.
  • Erasure (Art. 17) — request deletion of your account and orders. Note: on-chain transactions cannot be deleted; we can only remove our own DB records.
  • Restriction (Art. 18) — limit how we use your data while a dispute is resolved.
  • Portability (Art. 20) — receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format (we'll supply JSON).
  • Object (Art. 21) — to processing based on legitimate interest.
  • Complaint (Art. 77) — lodge a complaint with a competent data protection supervisory authority.

To exercise any of these rights, email privacy@owlorderfi.com from the wallet sign-in address or include a signed message from that wallet so we can verify the request is coming from the account owner.

8. Automated decision-making

We do not perform automated decision-making with legal or similarly significant effects. The keeper executes signed orders based on price triggers and schedules; it does not profile users or make discretionary decisions.

9. Children

The service is not directed at children under 16. We do not knowingly collect data from minors. If you believe a minor has used the service, contact us for deletion.

10. Changes to this policy

Material changes will be announced via a notice on the site at next sign-in. The "Last reviewed" date at the top of this page is updated whenever the text changes.
