How it works

Four ways to swap on your terms. Each is a single off-chain signature — the router enforces on-chain exactly what you signed, and a keeper bot pays the gas to execute it.

Limit

Set the price you'd accept and walk away. When the market reaches it, the keeper swaps for you — a better swap by waiting.

DCA

Dollar-cost average on a fixed schedule — hourly, daily, weekly — over a period you set. Sign once; the keeper buys each interval until the period ends or you cancel.

TWAP

Split one large order into N slices over a bounded window to target the average price and minimize market impact.

Ladder

Place a series of limit rungs across a price range to scale in or out gradually.

You stay self-custody the whole time. Tokens leave your wallet only in the single execution transaction, pulled via a standard ERC-20 allowance you can revoke at any time directly from your wallet.

FAQ

What is a keeper?

A bot that watches the chain and submits your signed order the moment its conditions are met. It pays the gas and can only do exactly what you signed — it can't change your price, your amount, or your minimum output.

Do you hold my funds?

No. The protocol is non-custodial. Your tokens stay in your wallet until the execution transaction, when they're pulled via a standard ERC-20 allowance you can revoke any time — independent of this site.

What does it cost?

A 0.30% protocol fee (dropping to 0.15% on larger orders), taken from the token you receive. No gas — the keeper pays it. Cancelling is free off-chain.

Which chains are supported?

Today: Base, Arbitrum and Optimism Sepolia testnets. Base mainnet is the next target. Swaps route through Uniswap V3 pools.

What can go wrong?

Smart-contract bugs, slippage and MEV (bounded by the minimum output you sign), keeper downtime (orders may not execute promptly during outages), and gas spikes (which can cause break-even skips). As with any DeFi contract, the worst-case loss is bounded by the ERC-20 allowance you've granted — typically the size of your open orders — and you can revoke or adjust allowances directly from your wallet at any time.
